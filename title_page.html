Medical MCQs - Psychiatry

Psychiatry Questions

1. Which of the following is a positive symptom of schizophrenia?

  • A. Hallucination
  • B. Delusion
  • C. A&B
  • D. Social isolation
  • E. Anhedonia

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Hallucinations are a positive symptom, but not the only one listed.
b. Incorrect: Delusions are a positive symptom, but not the only one listed.
c. Correct: Both hallucinations and delusions are positive symptoms of schizophrenia.
d. Incorrect: Social isolation is a negative symptom.
e. Incorrect: Anhedonia is a negative symptom.

2. The primary treatment for schizophrenia involves:

  • A. Antidepressants
  • B. Antipsychotic medications
  • C. Benzodiazepines
  • D. Stimulants
  • E. Anticonvulsants

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Antidepressants are not the primary treatment for psychosis.
b. Correct: Antipsychotic medications are the cornerstone of treatment for schizophrenia.
c. Incorrect: Benzodiazepines are used for short-term agitation, not as a primary treatment.
d. Incorrect: Stimulants can worsen psychosis.
e. Incorrect: Anticonvulsants are not first-line treatment for schizophrenia.

3. DSM 5 criteria for diagnosing generalized anxiety disorder is

  • A. 2 years
  • B. 6 months
  • C. 3 months
  • D. 1 month
  • E. Non of the above

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: 2 years is the duration for persistent depressive disorder.
b. Correct: DSM-5 requires excessive worry for at least 6 months for a GAD diagnosis.
c. Incorrect: 3 months is not the specific duration criterion for GAD.
d. Incorrect: 1 month is a duration criterion for other disorders like panic disorder or acute stress disorder.
e. Incorrect: The correct duration is listed as option B.

4. Which of the following traits is present in paranoid personality disorder:

  • A. seek for perfectionism
  • B. Rapid shifting of emotion
  • C. unstable relationships
  • D. general mistrust to others
  • E. Non of the above

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: Perfectionism is a trait of Obsessive-Compulsive Personality Disorder (OCPD).
b. Incorrect: Rapidly shifting emotion is characteristic of Borderline Personality Disorder.
c. Incorrect: Unstable relationships are characteristic of Borderline Personality Disorder.
d. Correct: The core feature of Paranoid Personality Disorder is pervasive mistrust and suspicion of others.
e. Incorrect: The correct trait is listed as option D.

5. The duration treatment of the 1st major depressive episode is

  • A. 2 years
  • B. 9-12 months
  • C. A&B
  • D. all of the above
  • E. non of the above

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: 2 years or more is considered maintenance therapy for recurrent or severe cases.
b. Correct: This timeframe covers the acute treatment phase plus a 4-9 month continuation phase to prevent relapse.
c. Incorrect: Option A is incorrect.
d. Incorrect: Option A is incorrect.
e. Incorrect: Option B is the standard recommendation.

6. Which of the following is negative psychotic feature;

  • A. Delusion
  • B. auditory hallucination
  • C. Disorganized speech
  • D. Anhedonia
  • E. Disorganized behavior

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: Delusions are a positive symptom.
b. Incorrect: Auditory hallucinations are a positive symptom.
c. Incorrect: Disorganized speech is a positive symptom.
d. Correct: Anhedonia (inability to feel pleasure) is a classic negative symptom.
e. Incorrect: Disorganized behavior is a positive symptom.

7. The most important feature (key) to diagnose manic episode is?

  • A. Anhedonia
  • B. Low mood
  • C. Elevated mood
  • D. all of the above
  • E. None of the above

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Anhedonia is a core feature of depression.
b. Incorrect: Low mood is the key feature of a depressive episode.
c. Correct: The cardinal feature of a manic episode is a distinct period of elevated, expansive, or irritable mood.
d. Incorrect: The options are contradictory.
e. Incorrect: The correct feature is listed.

8. Trichotillomania disorder is;

  • A. skin picking
  • B. Hair pulling
  • C. hoarding disorder
  • D. body dysmorphic disorder
  • E. All of the above

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Skin picking is excoriation disorder.
b. Correct: Trichotillomania is the irresistible urge to pull out one's own hair.
c. Incorrect: Hoarding disorder is a distinct diagnosis.
d. Incorrect: Body dysmorphic disorder involves perceived flaws in appearance.
e. Incorrect: Only option B is correct.

9. No purging (use of laxatives or induced vomiting) in

  • A. anorexia nervosa
  • B. Bulimia nervosa
  • C. Bing eating disorder
  • D. all of above
  • E. non of above

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Anorexia nervosa has a binge-eating/purging subtype.
b. Incorrect: Bulimia nervosa is defined by compensatory behaviors, often purging.
c. Correct: Binge eating disorder is characterized by binging without compensatory behaviors like purging.
d. Incorrect: Options A and B are incorrect.
e. Incorrect: Option C is correct.

10. Body mass index in anorexia nervosa is

  • A. Within average range
  • B. above average range
  • C. below average range
  • D. all of the above
  • E. A&B

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: A key criterion is significantly low body weight.
b. Incorrect: This is the opposite of anorexia nervosa.
c. Correct: By definition, individuals with anorexia have a BMI below the minimally normal range.
d. Incorrect: The options are mutually exclusive.
e. Incorrect: The options are mutually exclusive.

11. Which of the following disorders is an anxiety disorder?

  • A. Anorexia nervosa
  • B. Major depressive episode
  • C. social phobia
  • D. panic disorder
  • E. C&D

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect: Anorexia nervosa is an Eating Disorder.
b. Incorrect: Major depressive episode is part of a Depressive Disorder.
c. Incorrect: Social phobia is an anxiety disorder, but not the only one listed.
d. Incorrect: Panic disorder is an anxiety disorder, but not the only one listed.
e. Correct: Both social phobia (social anxiety disorder) and panic disorder are classified as anxiety disorders.

12. No psychotic features at all in ?

  • A. Mania
  • B. Schizophrenia
  • C. Hypomania
  • D. A&B
  • E. non of the above

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Severe mania can include psychotic features.
b. Incorrect: Schizophrenia is a primary psychotic disorder.
c. Correct: By definition, an episode is manic (not hypomanic) if psychotic features are present.
d. Incorrect: Both A and B can have psychotic features.
e. Incorrect: Option C is correct.

13. What are the clinical features of panic disorder?

  • A. tachycardia
  • B. shortness of breath
  • C. Fear of dying
  • D. all of the above
  • E. Non of the above

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: Tachycardia is a feature, but not the only one.
b. Incorrect: Shortness of breath is a feature, but not the only one.
c. Incorrect: Fear of dying is a feature, but not the only one.
d. Correct: Tachycardia, shortness of breath, and fear of dying are all classic features of a panic attack.
e. Incorrect: The listed options are all correct features.

14. Which of the following is a hallmark feature of Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)?

  • A. Social deficit
  • B. Low IQ
  • C. Lack of interest in physical appearance
  • D. Obsessive-compulsive traits
  • E. Language impairment

Correct answer: A
a. Correct: Persistent deficits in social communication and interaction are the core, defining feature of ASD.
b. Incorrect: Low IQ is a common comorbidity, not a core diagnostic feature.
c. Incorrect: This is not a diagnostic criterion.
d. Incorrect: While repetitive behaviors are a core feature, the social deficit is the primary hallmark.
e. Incorrect: Language impairment is not required for diagnosis; the key is the social use of language.

15. Which of the following is NOT included in the diagnostic criteria for ASD?

  • A. Persistent deficits in social communication
  • B. Repetitive behaviors or restricted interests
  • C. Language delay should be present
  • D. Symptoms must be present in early developmental period
  • E. Affect functioning

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: This is DSM-5 criterion A for ASD.
b. Incorrect: This is DSM-5 criterion B for ASD.
c. Correct: Language delay is common but is NOT a required diagnostic criterion.
d. Incorrect: This is DSM-5 criterion C for ASD.
e. Incorrect: Deficits in affect functioning are part of criterion A.

16. The core symptoms of ADHD include:

  • A. Social communication impairment
  • B. Impulsivity, hyperactivity, and inattention
  • C. Repetitive behaviors and restricted interests
  • D. Hallucinations and delusions
  • E. Anxiety and depression

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: This is a core feature of ASD.
b. Correct: ADHD is defined by a persistent pattern of inattention and/or hyperactivity-impulsivity.
c. Incorrect: These are core features of ASD.
d. Incorrect: These are symptoms of psychosis.
e. Incorrect: These are common comorbidities, not core symptoms of ADHD.

17. At what age must symptoms of ADHD typically begin for a diagnosis to be made?

  • A. Before the age of 5 years
  • B. Before the age of 12 years
  • C. Before the age of 18 years
  • D. At any age

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: The diagnostic threshold is higher.
b. Correct: According to DSM-5, several symptoms must be present before age 12.
c. Incorrect: This is too late for the onset criteria.
d. Incorrect: ADHD is a neurodevelopmental disorder with childhood onset.

18. The first-line pharmacological treatment for ADHD is:

  • A. Antidepressants
  • B. Stimulants such as methylphenidate
  • C. Antipsychotics
  • D. Benzodiazepines
  • E. Antiepileptics

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Certain antidepressants are second or third-line options.
b. Correct: Stimulant medications are the most effective and first-line treatment for ADHD.
c. Incorrect: Antipsychotics are not used for core ADHD symptoms.
d. Incorrect: Benzodiazepines are not used for ADHD.
e. Incorrect: Antiepileptics are not a standard treatment for ADHD.

19. Adaptive functioning deficits in Intellectual Disability include difficulties in:

  • A. Social, conceptual, and practical domains
  • B. Only cognitive domains
  • C. Only academic performance
  • D. Emotional regulation
  • E. Non of the above

Correct answer: A
a. Correct: Diagnosis requires deficits in all three adaptive domains: conceptual, social, and practical.
b. Incorrect: Deficits must also be present in social and practical domains.
c. Incorrect: Academic performance is only one part of the conceptual domain.
d. Incorrect: This is part of the broader domains, not a standalone pillar.
e. Incorrect: Option A is correct.

20. Which of the following is a common feature of OCD?

  • A. Persistent hallucinations
  • B. Intrusive and distressing thoughts
  • C. Persistent mood swings
  • D. Paranoid delusions
  • E. Memory loss

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Hallucinations are features of psychotic disorders.
b. Correct: This describes obsessions, which are a core feature of OCD.
c. Incorrect: Mood swings are more characteristic of mood disorders.
d. Incorrect: Paranoid delusions are features of psychotic disorders.
e. Incorrect: Memory loss is not a core feature of OCD.

21. In OCD, compulsions are:

  • A. Irrational fears
  • B. Physical symptoms of anxiety
  • C. Repetitive behaviors aimed at reducing distress
  • D. Fixed false beliefs
  • E. Sudden mood changes

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Irrational fears are more aligned with phobias.
b. Incorrect: These are the result of obsessions, which compulsions aim to alleviate.
c. Correct: Compulsions are repetitive acts performed to reduce the anxiety caused by obsessions.
d. Incorrect: Fixed false beliefs are delusions.
e. Incorrect: Sudden mood changes are not a defining feature of compulsions.

22. Which of the following criteria is NOT part of diagnosing substance use disorder according to DSM-5?

  • A. Tolerance
  • B. Withdrawal symptoms
  • C. Continued use despite health consequences
  • D. Experiencing euphoria
  • E. Spending significant time obtaining the substance

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: Tolerance is a diagnostic criterion.
b. Incorrect: Withdrawal is a diagnostic criterion.
c. Incorrect: Continued use despite negative consequences is a diagnostic criterion.
d. Correct: Experiencing euphoria is an effect of a drug, not a criterion for the disorder.
e. Incorrect: Spending significant time on the substance is a diagnostic criterion.

23. Which of the following is the most common sleep disorder in the general population?

  • A. Narcolepsy
  • B. Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA)
  • C. Insomnia
  • D. Restless Legs Syndrome (RLS)
  • E. Parasomnias

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Narcolepsy is relatively rare.
b. Incorrect: OSA is common, but insomnia is generally considered more prevalent.
c. Correct: Insomnia disorder is the most prevalent sleep disorder.
d. Incorrect: RLS is less common than insomnia.
e. Incorrect: Parasomnias are less common than insomnia.

24. Normal sleep requirement is

  • A. 10 hr
  • B. 12 hr
  • C. A or B
  • D. 7.5 hr
  • E. non of the above

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: 10 hours is on the high end for adults.
b. Incorrect: 12 hours is excessive for most adults.
c. Incorrect: Both options are outside the typical range for adults.
d. Correct: This falls squarely within the recommended 7-9 hours of sleep for adults.
e. Incorrect: Option D is a reasonable answer.

25. Which of the following best describes the current perspective of homosexuality in psychiatry according to the DSM-5?

  • A. Homosexuality is classified as a mental disorder.
  • B. Homosexuality is considered a normal variation of human sexuality.
  • C. Homosexuality is categorized as a personality disorder.
  • D. Homosexuality is associated with psychosis.
  • E. None of the above

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Homosexuality was removed from the DSM in 1973.
b. Correct: The modern consensus is that homosexuality is a normal variant of human sexuality.
c. Incorrect: It is not a personality disorder.
d. Incorrect: There is no association between homosexuality and psychosis.
e. Incorrect: Option B is correct.

26. Suicide attempts are more common in ?

  • A. Males
  • B. Females
  • C. Males and females have equal risk
  • D. non of the above
  • E. All of the above

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Males have higher rates of completed suicide.
b. Correct: Females have a higher rate of suicide attempts.
c. Incorrect: There is a clear gender disparity.
d. Incorrect: Option B is correct.
e. Incorrect: The options are mutually exclusive.

27. Which of the following is a core symptom of major depressive disorder?

  • A. Elevated mood
  • B. Low mood
  • C. Increased energy
  • D. Delusions and hallucinations
  • E. All of the above

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Elevated mood is a symptom of mania.
b. Correct: Low mood (depressed mood) is a cardinal symptom of MDD.
c. Incorrect: Increased energy is a symptom of mania.
d. Incorrect: Psychotic features can be present but are not a core symptom for diagnosis.
e. Incorrect: The options are contradictory.

28. Which of the following is NOT a common symptom of major depressive disorder?

  • A. Suicidal ideation
  • B. Anhedonia
  • C. Low energy
  • D. High mood
  • E. Feelings of guilt

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: Suicidal ideation is a common symptom of MDD.
b. Incorrect: Anhedonia is a core symptom of MDD.
c. Incorrect: Low energy or fatigue is a common symptom of MDD.
d. Correct: High mood is characteristic of mania, the opposite of depression.
e. Incorrect: Feelings of guilt are a common symptom of MDD.

29. Which of the following is a potential complication of untreated depression?

  • A. Enhanced cognitive function
  • B. Improved relationships
  • C. Increased risk of suicide
  • D. Enhanced immune function
  • E. Decreased pain sensitivity

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Depression commonly causes cognitive dysfunction.
b. Incorrect: Depression often strains relationships.
c. Correct: Untreated depression is a major risk factor for suicide.
d. Incorrect: Depression is associated with immune system suppression.
e. Incorrect: Depression is often associated with increased pain sensitivity.

30. A 25-year-old woman presents to your clinic complaining of intense fear and anxiety in social situations, especially when she has to speak in front of groups. She worries excessively about being judged or embarrassed, leading her to avoid social gatherings. She has experienced these symptoms for several years. Based on this description, which of the following is the most likely diagnosis?

  • A. Generalized Anxiety Disorder
  • B. Social Anxiety Disorder (Social Phobia)
  • C. Panic Disorder
  • D. Obsessive compulsive disorder
  • E. Major Depressive Disorder

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: GAD involves worry about a wide range of topics, not just social situations.
b. Correct: The symptoms described are the classic presentation of Social Anxiety Disorder.
c. Incorrect: Panic disorder involves unexpected panic attacks and worry about future attacks.
d. Incorrect: OCD involves obsessions and compulsions, which are not described.
e. Incorrect: The core feature described is fear of social judgment, not low mood.
